Fear Setting Guide

'Fear-setting' is a process for defining your fears, and discovering how you can move forward from them. By helping you express what you value and what's at stake, fear setting can help walk you through the ambiguity of a difficult decision.

*Fear setting was made famous by Tim Ferriss in his TED talk.
You can read more about it here.

Overview

How does it work?

Choose a fear to work through as you go through the following 5 stages:

  • Define — What's the worst that could happen?
  • Prevent — How can you prevent this from happening?
  • Repair — If the worst happens, what can you do to overcome it?
  • Benefits — What's worth doing even if you fail?
  • Costs — What are the costs of doing nothing?

Begin

Ready to get started?

Choose a fear to work through and then follow the steps in order, first by reflecting on the question in italics, then completing the exercise below it.

It can be especially helpful to write down your responses to each exercise—that way you'll have a finished project you can turn to later.

Preparation

Name your fear

What am I afraid will happen?

Exercise Write down the fear you'd like to work through. Then write down how afraid you are of taking this step, on a scale of 1–10.

Step 1 of 5

Define

What's the worst that could happen?

Exercise List the potential worst case scenarios that could happen if you do what you fear.

Step 2 of 5

Prevent

How can I avoid this?

Exercise For every worst case scenario you listed above, write down what you can do to prevent them from happening, or how you can minimize their occurrence.

Step 3 of 5

Repair

If the worst happens, how can I fix it?

Exercise For every worst case scenario listed above, write down what you could do to overcome the experience if it happens. Consider what you can do to fix what's broken or how you can minimize the damage.

Step 4 of 5

Benefits

What's worth doing even if I fail?

Exercise List some of the benefits of doing what you fear. What might change in your life if you have a full or partial success?

Step 5 of 5

Costs

What are the costs of inaction?

Think about the costs of staying in your current situation. Write down what could happen if you don't take action soon:

Exercise
  • If I do nothing, in 6 months…
  • If I do nothing, in one year…
  • If I do nothing, in three years…

Reflect

Reflection

Now that you've completed the exercise, reflect on what you've written. Does the fear seem as scary as it did before, now that you know you can prevent or recover from the worst case scenarios?

As you move forward, remember that fear is often in our heads. You'll never know if your fears are well-founded until you face them. Until you take the chance, until you go out and see, you'll always wonder.
